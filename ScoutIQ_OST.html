Desired Outcome
Sporting Directors at mid-tier European clubs make faster, better-informed transfer decisions
Reduce the gap between raw player data and actionable insight — from days of manual analysis to a single, contextualised evaluation session.

Broken down into
Opportunities
Pain Point 1
Fragmented data sources
Clubs simultaneously work with Wyscout, scout PDF reports, and Excel. No synthesis layer exists — the Sporting Director must manually integrate everything.
Workflow Data
Pain Point 2
Lack of tactical context
Data shows absolute stats but not whether the player fits the club's playing system. Stats without context produce poor decisions.
Insight Tactical
Pain Point 3
Long decision cycles
Evaluating a transfer candidate takes 2–3 days. Transfer windows are short and competitive — slow decisions mean missed signings.
Speed Efficiency
Pain Point 4
Scout bias
Decisions rely too heavily on the subjective perception of individual observers. Million-euro calls need more than one person's opinion.
Objectivity Risk

Metric
⭐ North Star Metric
Insight-to-Decision Rate > 30%
Target: More than 30% of Fit Score profiles viewed by the Sporting Director result in an active shortlist action — within the first transfer window after launch.

This metric bridges "data consumed" and "decision made". It is the only metric that directly validates the core hypothesis: that the platform's insights translate into real transfer decisions, not just passive engagement.
